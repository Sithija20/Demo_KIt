Our Project

The Mechanism Demonstrator Kit is a university project aimed at creating an interactive and educational tool for school and university students. The primary goal is to provide a hands-on experience for understanding fundamental mechanical principles through tangible interaction.

This kit allows students to assemble, disassemble, and experiment with various common mechanisms, including:

  • Gear and Pinion sets ('Involute' and 'Cycloidal' teeth)
  • 'Simple' and 'Compound' Gear Trains
  • 'Flat Belt' and Pulley systems (demonstrating 'Belt Tension')
  • 'CAM' mechanisms (allowing graphing of 'Follower' motion)

This accompanying website serves as a comprehensive guide, providing assembly instructions, theoretical explanations, experiment procedures, and further resources to enhance the learning experience.
